▎ Before you sign up

Quick questions.

How often is Mason sent?
Twice a week. Monday at 7am your time (The Build) and Thursday at 7am your time (The Move). Every week, no skips. If we ever go on break, you'll know in advance — we don't just ghost.
Is it really free?
Yes, the newsletter is free forever. We make money on the consulting side — building AI systems and automations for teams, founders, and individuals who want the thing done right. Mason itself stays free because a good newsletter is the clearest way to show how we think about the work. A paid tier is on the roadmap eventually (archives, deep-dives, playbook vault) — and early subscribers will be grandfathered into free access when it launches.
What's the catch with the "occasional pitch"?
No catch — just being honest. Roughly once a month we'll run an issue about our own consulting work. Clearly marked, never mid-story. Most issues will be exactly what you signed up for: real people, real prompts, real systems. If you don't want the occasional pitch, one-click unsubscribe works every time.
Who writes it?
A small team that's been shipping AI systems for companies and individuals over the past few years. Each issue is researched, source-verified, and fact-checked before it ships. No ghostwritten garbage, no AI-generated slop, no content farms.
How is this different from every other AI newsletter?
Most AI newsletters summarize press releases. Mason tracks what people actually ship. We name the builder, link the source, show the prompt, list what broke. Every issue has to leave you with one concrete thing you didn't know before — or it doesn't ship.
Can I read past issues before subscribing?
Yes — scroll up to the Recent Issues section, or hit the archive link. No paywall, no signup required to read. Subscribe once you know it's worth your inbox.
What do you do with my email?
We send you Mason. That's it. We never sell, share, or rent your email to anyone. Your role / company size / country data helps us write better issues over time — it's not shared externally. One-click unsubscribe removes you completely.
